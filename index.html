A. Abstract

In this paper, we propose an entire pipeline for the trajectory planning of robotic manipulators to address the cleaning task of 3D-Printed objects. Based on the mesh model of the printed part, we present a new approach to efficiently generate coverage lines that can achieve a lower time complexity and approximation ratio than the baseline through imposing cost constraints, defining reference points, and separating the computing process. The advantage of our approach is significantly demonstrated for meshes with massive numbers of triangles. The coverage path of the whole mesh is then computed by using the 2-Opt algorithm with imposing additional cost constraints. In addition, we propose an optimization-based common margin to support finding a set of collision-free and optimal robotic configurations from the coverage path. Then, the final collision-free robot trajectories subjected to robotic constraints are computed. Comparative simulations and practical experiments are carried out to demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach.

C. Physical cleaning demonstration

We conducted a practical cleaning demonstration for object B with the following setup: Nx = Ny = 13, θm = 30 (deg), the blasting pressure is set at 60 psi, cleaning time ~300 sec (at 95% planned speed). A NEMA17 stepper motor is used to hold and flip the 3D-printed part.
